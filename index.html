Work Order System Web Design

About the Project

SkySpecs is an aerospace company deploys autonomous drones to provide safe, affordable, and automated wind turbine inspections for the WindEnergy Industry. We built a work order system for SkySpecs Drone to streamline their workflow. This is a website that can be fully utilized by SkySpecs Emploees and their customers to record, check and share information of inspections, customers and turbines, etc.

My Role

My role in this team is user researcher and UX/UI designer. I work closely with another designer and the web development subteam. I designed the information architecture and came up with work flows and interface layouts.

SkySpecs Drone.

Problem Statement

The workflow of SkySpecs includes 6 steps can be seen in figure 1. They receive a request by phone, schedule via Google Calendar and take notes via spreadsheet (haven't used it for a while), etc. However, there is only one web portal for the 5th step to store a collection of uploaded data and annotation, which means for the rest of the process there is no platform/system to track records or check progress. With the growth of the company, customers and corresponding inspections are increasing which necessitates a system to streamline their workflow and run business systematically and more efficiently.


figure 1. SkySpecs Workflow

Users & Stakeholders

We have performed research with our client's management and operation team to ideate, validate concepts and gather feedback. The Work Order System is used for both SkySpecs crew as well as their customers but they have different access levels. As figure 3, 4, 5 below shows there are three different primary users in SkySpecs: director, drone operator and data analyst. And their customers are the secondary users of this system, as shown in figure 6. Therefore, designing for primary users has the highest priority. The major responsibility of the director is to schedule with their customers and assign inspections to the operator.

figure 4.Operator Persona

The responsibility of the operator is to do inspections on wind farms and upload data. And what the analyst are responsible for is annotating the data in the web portal. Therefore in the work order system, they should have hierarchical access to different sections of this system.

Solution Overview

As you can see in figure 6, there are four main sections in work order system: work order information, customer information, turbine information and agenda. The presentation of the information is using tables, which can contain all records. When a request comes in, users can add a new record and finish 4 steps to the confirmation. Users can check their events in agenda and change the inspection status later.


figure 6.SkySpecs Work Order System Structure

Design Process

We work closely with our stakeholders and update our progress each week. Therefore, we can gather instant feedback, change our pivot and refine our design efficiently.

Initial Design


1.Prioritization: According to stakeholders' requirements, we prioritized their needs: schedule inspections > calendar integration > ticket system > inventory tracking > weather integration > shortest path caculation > order history analysis > order prioritization > notification system > quote generation.

2.Compararive Evaluation: To gain insights of how does work order system function, we did the comparative evaluation of existing work order platforms: NetFacilities, eMaint, etc. We also did the analogous comparison with scheduling systems: Google Calendar, Doodle, etc. For the overall layout of this system, we chose to keep it consistent with the looks of SkySpecs existing web portal. Design Iterations


At the earlier phase, we only separated users into SkySpecs crew and their customer, which was too general. To better understand their expectations, we interviewed people from different teams -- management, development, technique, etc. And we came up with three more accurate personas that we should design for: they are directors, drone operators, and data analysts. Therefore, we determined our first round of iteration would be focusing on the first persona -- SkySpecs directors.

"We always phone back and forth for many times to schedule, because there are too many conditions we have to consider."

1.No more scheduling system: Client's requirements are always changing. As the quote shows above, our client thought the scheduling process was too complicated to be done simply in a system, so they would not finalize the inspection date just for one single time. Instead, they prefer a system that can simplify the subsequent workflow, after the inspection dates have been scheduled. We reconsider the structure of the system: the information should be encompassed in one work order and the relevant steps, as shown below.

2."Assign to" feature: One of the subsequent work is to assign inspections to operators. I designed a pop-up window to simplify interaction.

3.Customer information table: We added a customer section in the global navigation bar. And its main content is a customer table. Users can check customers information here or direct to add-new-customer progress by clicking on the button in the add-new-work-order progress. 4.Turbine information table: We added a turbine section in the global navigation bar. And its main content is a turbine table. 5.Agenda: We moved the calendar from the previous place (scheduling system) to a separate section in global navigation, where users can switch between calendar view and agenda view. The event will be added to agenda automatically once the creation of a new work order is done.

BooksMap Mobile App Design

About the Project

In this project, I prototyped an application that enables students at the University of Michigan to find inexpensive used books or sell unneeded books to others. This application ahs three unique features: using a map to locate nearby users and facilitate contact and know them; providing 2 options for users to post or search information via Have or Need ; paying with money or creative ways such as a dinner or a movie, etc.
See Paper Prototype.
Audience
Students at the University of Michigan
Practice Areas

UX/UI Design.
Information Architecture.
Storyboarding.
Interaction Design.

Problem Statement

Students often find books through three methods: borrowing in libraries, buying in bookstores or purchasing online. However, books in libraries are often in short supply, those in bookstores might be expensive, and it takes time for shipping if purchased online. What if students want to get books instantly and at low cost? At the same time, there are some students who want to get rid of unneeded books. So how could those students connect with each other?

Solution Overview

I am providing students access to inexpensive books while providing others an outlet for their unneeded books. I designed an application which allows both groups to share the information of books they have/want, prices they feel comfortable with and a good location to exchange the books. This application allows them to reach the nearest users and connect with them.
This application works best when there are a lot of users, so I decided to firstly narrow down the community to just students on campus, where a lot of students live. To avoid the problem of users not finding books and never coming back to use this APP again, I give users alternative option to post their needs first. Additionally, in order to attract more users and make this platform interesting, I will add one feature that users can pay with money or creative ways such as a dinner or a movie, etc.

Walkthrough

Users can sell or purchase books through two main methods: one is their interests and the other is searching on the map. During this process, they can sort the information by time, by price or by proximity in the dropdown menu.

Users will be asked to select their topics of interest when registering. This information is used to tailor their homepage. On the homepage, users can scroll down to see all interests or swipe to see all the books under this interest. Of course, they can edit and check each.   (click on images)

Design Process

Initial Design.


The initial design of my application also had a function that allows users to rent and borrow books from libraries that have a bookshelf for used books and a librarian who is in charge. Information on books is also provided on the APP together with proximity. This application also provides book information from libraries and bookstores as alternatives to compare prices and quality at the same time. This idea was generated from one of my sketches (Figure 1).


Figure 1. Sketch in 8 solutions storyboard

Critical Features.


  • •  Directly connect users who need books with those who can provide idled second-hand books

  • •  Provide information retrieving feature of Second-hand Books Library

  • •  Provide information of the same books from school libraries and bookstores

Reflection.


According to the reading the Design of Everyday Things by Donald A.Norman, well-designed products should be clearly used with fewer instructions or even without any text-based information. Just the visual, acoustic information themselves, being consistent with as many as possible of the four constraints, are enough to guide users to utilize them properly. I cant agree more on Donalds standpoint, so consider it as my most important design philosophy.
However, it is hard to achieve. After I built my paper prototypes(Figure 2 & 3) and showed them to my peers, they felt really confusedabout each feature, even though I explained all the time. Good design should be introductions the products themselves, but I explained a lot because they were way too much complex.


Figure 2. Too much content on one page and a lot of text near each button


Figure 3. Crowed layout makes users hard to understand

Changes.


Its easy to get lost when designing the features of an application, adding tedious details and redundant function buttons or pages, which I think would be necessary for users. But, thats not true, because thats the application design for designer myself, not users.
Only this one feature- share books with peers- already keeps this application complex, therefore, I decide to abandon the idea of Second-hand Books Library and keep the feature of compare with other resources as one simple button. I made this decision is not only because it follows my design philosophy, but also because a lot of target users and peers think its unnecessary.
After showing my paper prototypes to my peers and target users, I got a lot of feedbacks from them and added those buttons or indicators which I think proper right after they recommended. For example, I added a slider on the homepage (Figure 4) and I changed the drawer-shaped menu to hamburger menu (Figure 5), which takes less space and wouldnt cause confusion.


Figure 4. Homepage Sketch


Figure 5. Want page


Figure 6. Have page
One main change that I made, which was suggested by several users, was I added one page to show all the user who have/want this book when clicking the book image on homepage, instead of leading to individual book page which posted by one user each time. Because of this change, I simultaneously moved some buttons (Figure 4.location button and user profile button) from homepage to this page (Figure 5 & 6), making homepage much more clean and simple.
Another main change that I made was I added Want or Have (Figure 5 & 6) option for users. It is hard to let this application work if there are only a few users because only if there are enough users, there would be enough books. Imagine if you wanted to find one book on this App, but found there was no one posted any information about this, you would feel frustrated. But if you have tried several times, things are still the same, you would feel disappointed and never turn back. In order to avoid this happening, I provide users the option of choosing Want as your purpose, which means even if there is no one Have this book right now, users can post needs first and let others search their needs.

File Management Consulting for Student Academic Affairs (SAA)

In this project, our team came up recommendations for Student Academic Affairs (SAA) in the University of Michigan College of Literature, Science and Arts (LSA) to alleviate the file management problem. We broke down the problem into 3 parts: technology (how to use Google Drive and M+ Box), naming protocol and implementation. My role was diving into the implementation section, finding out reasons why staff in SAA not following naming protocel and proposed recommendations.
See SAA website.
Client
UMichigan LSA Student Academic Affairs Office.
Skill
Contextual Inquiry.
User-Centered Design
Tool
Affinity Wall.

Problem Description

LSA Student Academic Affairs has multiple locations and mediums for storage of information (email, attachments, Google Drive, M+Box, internal websites, external websites), which makes it difficult to find information, as well as ensure information is consistent across all mediums. LSA Student Academic Affairs would like help in developing a process/guideline for how to store information. The current process is not being adhered to by the employees due to ease of use, lack of accountability, and lack of a logical process flow.


Methodological Overview:



Collect Data.


    •  Interviews
  •   ·  Ask Questions
  •   ·  Observing Workflows
    •  Background Research
  •   ·  M+Box
  •   ·  Google Drive
  •   ·  Naming Protocol
  •   ·  Rule Adherence

Analyze Data.


    •  Interpretation Session
  •   ·  Review Interviews
  •   ·  Take Annotated Notes

Synthesize Data.


    •  Affinity Wall
  •   ·  Affinity Notes
  •   ·  Walkthrough Sessions
    •  Feedback
    •  Brainstorm

Key Findings

Key Finding 1:

Staff feel protocols dont reflect their needs and perspective.


  •   •  A good idea Ill never use
  •   •  A perception that top-down protocols are ineffective or irrelevant
  •   •  Lack of voice in design process
  •   •  Staff use ad hoc file management strategies they find personally useful, and that are not driven by the 2014 protocol

Key Finding 2:

Users lack understanding and training


  •   •  Staff relied heavily on Google Drive for their everyday workflows, but most struggled to understand its features and behaviors.
  •   •  M+Box is a distant-feeling system that staff feel uncomfortable using
  •   •  Confusion about how to approach file management & file migration from old systems

Key Finding 3:

Organizational change resistance


  •   •  Continued shadow use of Shared Drive by some users
  •   •  Cultural change resistance and reliance on old systems
  •   •  Confusion about how to approach file management & file migration from old systems

Key Recommendations

A User-Centered Redesign


This user-centered redesign can be broken up into three categories: Structural/Design Recomendations; Implementational Recomendations and Organizational Recomendations.


Design Recomendations.


  •   •  Use of a single Google Drive Structure.
Google drive can be set up for an entire organization to operate with one structure. This will concretize the structure and consistency of folder names and locations; avoid confusion over multiple individuals filing systems; allow for administration from a single point or individual and inherently standardize structure and look.


  •   •  Streamlined File Naming Convention
Simplify the file naming convention: reduce from 5 components to 2 components. Simpler is better even if it conveys less information due to the fact that it will be used period. Complexity leads to no use, inconsistency to no value.


Implementational Recomendations.


  •   •  Engage Users in Redesign Process.
Engage all employees throughout the design and implementation processes. Do user research and needs assessment and show this process as a benefit, not a burden. When people feel their needs have been considered and they feel that they are part of a change effort that change is much more likely to be permanent.


  •   •  Change Implementation Strategy.
  •   ·  Unfreeze: Communicate change process, necessity of change
  •   ·  Change: Implement change, engage users, abate change resistence and garner feedback.
  •   ·  Refreeze: Reward change champions and monitor outcomes.


Kurt Lewins 3 Stage Change Model

Organizational Recomendations.


  •   •  System Administrator(s).
We recognize that it may not be possible to hire on someone for this job. However we do believe that these responsbilities assigned to a single or more would significantly assist with non-adherence: Manage Permissions, Monitor Adherence, Design the file structure, Continuous Improvement.


  •   •  Training and Superusers.
Post design we suggest the following training elements:
  •   ·  Provide initial training session to educate team on Google Drive best practices, permissions, functionality.
  •   ·  Secondary training on organizational Google Drive structure.
  •   ·  Create or reinstate super-users at the team level.
  •   •  One Page Printed Reference Guide.
  •   ·  Include Administrator and Team Expert Contacts.
  •   ·  Visualization of single Drive Structure.
  •   ·  Quick Reference with example of File Naming Convention.


Conclusion

LSA SAA is a student centered organization dedicated to the development and growth of undergraduate students at University of Michigan. This function will always come first for the LSA SAA, therefore processes in place to support student interaction must use the least complex solutions to work well for all staff members. Existing file management procedures have faltered due to the complexity of protocols and the manner in which the last change iteration was rolled out. The existing design iteration is not supported by users in the office because they feel their needs have heard; as a result, staff feel estranged by the change process as opposed to an integral part of it. To create a new file management system that addresses these problems we recommend LSA SAA focus on user research in the next design iteration and implement our streamlined design recommendations centered on reducing complexity and redundancies within file management protocols. By utilizing these suggestions LSA SAA can better integrate file management into the workflow of users, garner ground-up support for change, and create processes that connect the potential value of the system to everyday use.

Bike Down Memory Lane

In this project, our team utilized Visual Reality to provide realistic expeience of cycling indoors. Audio, vison and sense of touch came one by one. Fisrt, people can only hear the sound and when they start pedaling they can see, hear and feel the wind and environment at the sam time. Tp achieve these, we used Arduino, TouchDesigner, Oculus, Accelerometer and bike with trainer.

Motivation

People nowadays spend too much time indoors, in front of computers, working and studying, so our design motivation was to let people experience indoor cycling and motivate them to go outside for some fresh air and exercises.

Design Process

We first brainstormed how we can recall peoples memory and what senses do we need to make a memory realistic. According to Condillacs Statue, we wanted to start with sense of smell and finally sense of touch with an installation let people experience each senses in sequence. But then we figured out we wanted something more realistic, so we decided to use Visual Reality.
However, we wanted to engage participants in different senses one by one, which cannot be achieved only by VR. So we came up with an idea using bicycle and show them cycling scenes.
After finalizing the theme of our project, we started building the storyline and breaking down the project into VR, audio and video aspects.

Timeline.


So our storyline was: first, participants put on headset and earphones when they can only see darkness but hear people talking and sounds in the environment; and then they will be led to the bicycle and get on it; When they start pedaling, they will see the downhill scenes and people cycling beside them; at the same time, we will use a table fan to provide them the feeling of wind and use accelerometer to send their movement to Arduino providing real time speed, which makes the speed more realistic and refreshing.


Sketche of Timeline.

Components.


I chose 3 logotypes from my sketches and created them in Adobe Illustrator. I used Segoe Script for those logos, becasue the script is stylish. Since the customer wanted something simple but fasionable, I chose a fancy typeface to accompany the simple logo, each logo involved a simple design. If both were simple, it would be dull.

My Role

VR Vedio Maker.


My role in this project was focusing on video making.
First, I searched 360 degree videos in high quality for VR online and found the Trike Drifting video in 360 degrees that we used later on. And then I edited this video in Adobe Premiere, adding darkness prior to the video for only audio echoing without vision, cutting down useless parts, extending the length and making the whole video smooth. After that, I combined the audio into the video and used Spatial Media Metadata Injector to make this video 360 degrees for VR use.

Arduino Coder.


In the process of bringing those parts together, I also helped with the coding in Arduino. The video playing speed was so sensitive to the original speed of the pedaling, so every real time speed was sent to the TouchDesigner, making the video unstable. So we decided to only use the peak values for one rotating of the pedal, making the speed less sensitive and stable. I helped writing the code in Arduino to extract only peak values as outputs to control the speed of video playing.
